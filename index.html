Did You Know?

Taxi Fares Reimbursement

Taxi Fares Reimbursement is a service offered by some car insurance policies where the insurance company covers the cost of taxi fares when your car is unavailable due to a covered event, such as an accident or repair.

Key Points:

  • Coverage Limits: There is usually a maximum amount or limit per day or per incident.
  • Conditions: The reimbursement typically applies only when the car is being repaired for a covered claim.
  • Claims Process: To claim this benefit, you may need to provide receipts for the taxi fares incurred.

Towing

Towing coverage is a part of car insurance that pays for the cost of towing your vehicle to a repair shop after an accident or breakdown.

Key Points:

  • Coverage Limits: There may be limits on how far your vehicle can be towed (e.g., 100 miles).
  • Conditions: It generally covers towing to the nearest qualified repair facility.
  • Additional Services: Sometimes, towing coverage is bundled with roadside assistance, which can include services like jump-starting a dead battery, changing a flat tire, or delivering fuel.

Car Renting Charges Reimbursement

Car Renting Charges Reimbursement is a service that provides coverage for rental car expenses while your vehicle is being repaired due to a covered incident.

Key Points:

  • Coverage Limits: There are typically daily limits (e.g., 300 AED per day) and a total limit (e.g., up to 3000 AED).
  • Conditions: The reimbursement is available only for the period your car is in the shop for repairs after a covered claim.
  • Rental Options: Some insurance companies have agreements with specific rental car companies, or they may allow you to choose any rental service.

Agency Maintenance

Agency Maintenance refers to regular, scheduled maintenance services provided by authorized dealerships or service centers affiliated with the car's manufacturer.

Key Points:

  • Benefits: Ensures that the vehicle is maintained according to the manufacturer's specifications, which can help preserve the warranty.
  • Coverage: This is not typically covered by standard car insurance but may be part of an extended warranty or maintenance plan purchased separately.
  • Services Included: May include oil changes, tire rotations, brake inspections, and other routine maintenance tasks.

Agency Repair

Agency Repair refers to repair services performed by authorized dealerships or service centers that are approved by the car's manufacturer.

Key Points:

  • Benefits: Repairs are done according to manufacturer standards, often using genuine parts.
  • Coverage: Many comprehensive car insurance policies offer this as an option or include it for a certain period.
  • Conditions: Typically applies to newer vehicles and may be limited to a certain number of years or mileage.

FAQs

FAQs provide answers to common questions about car insurance policies, coverage options, claims process, and other related topics.

Common FAQs:

  • What is covered under a comprehensive car insurance policy?

    Comprehensive coverage usually includes protection against theft, vandalism, natural disasters, fire, and damage to the car from a collision.

  • How do I file a claim?

    Contact your insurance provider, provide details of the incident, submit any required documents, and follow their instructions for inspection and repairs.

  • Will my insurance premium increase after a claim?

    It depends on the insurer and the nature of the claim. Some insurers offer accident forgiveness, which means your first claim might not affect your premium.

  • Can I choose my repair shop?

    Many insurers have preferred repair networks, but you may have the option to choose your own. Check your policy for specifics.

  • What is a deductible?

    A deductible is the amount you pay out of pocket before your insurance covers the rest of the claim. Higher deductibles can lower your premium.
